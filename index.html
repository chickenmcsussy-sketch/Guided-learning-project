04 · Interviews

Interview Questions

Dad

Software · Firewalls · Network Security
  1. When you first started working with computers, what did you find most confusing about how everything connected — hardware, software, and networking?
  2. From a security standpoint, how much does the physical hardware layer actually matter — does the type of network card or router affect firewall performance?
  3. Can you walk me through how you troubleshoot a system issue, like a firewall behaving unexpectedly?
  4. Are there hardware limitations or vulnerabilities that people on the software side tend to overlook?
  5. How has the relationship between hardware and software changed over the years?
  6. What advice would you give a computer engineering student trying to connect hardware knowledge to the real-world software side?

Lecturer's Friend

Hardware Specialist
  1. How did you first get into the hardware side of computers, and what drew you to it over software?
  2. If you were building a computer from scratch today, where would you start and what would your process look like?
  3. What do you think is the most underappreciated component in a computer, and why?
  4. How would you describe the relationship between the CPU, RAM, and motherboard to someone just starting out?
  5. Are there common misconceptions about hardware that people coming from a software background tend to have?
  6. How much do you think hardware knowledge matters for someone going into software or computer engineering today?
  7. What resources have helped you most in deepening your hardware knowledge?
